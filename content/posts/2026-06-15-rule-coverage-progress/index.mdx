As Warhammer 40,000 moves into its 11th edition, the question I get most is _how much of the new 
rules does Fatecaster actually model?_ To answer it with real numbers rather than a hand-wave, 
I built a multi-agent workflow that audits the entire catalog — every unit ability, army rule, 
detachment rule, and stratagem — against our live damage engine. The latest pass, run on 15 June, 
looked at more than 6,800 distinct rules and abilities across 43 factions and 133 detachments. 
Of those, about 2,880 actually affect damage calculations; the rest govern movement, objectives, 
command points, and other effects that sit outside the attack math we are currently modeling.

Of the content that affects damage, 63% is fully modeled and 93% is at least partially modeled
— meaning the core effect of the rule is already in the math, even where a narrow sub-clause may
be intentionally ignored, approximated, simplified or just still being refined. That leaves roughly
200 effects (about 7%) not yet modeled and sitting in the review queue. Put plainly: when you analyze
a list in Fatecaster, the overwhelming majority of the rules that change the result are already being
accounted for. The remaining 7% are some difficult logic cases that need manual modelling decisions.

Encouragingly, many of the partially modelled rules have not been genuine modeling gaps; most of 
the items are either intentionally simplified or missing some hard to model conditions that don't have a 
large blast radius. Just as an example, we don't care whether a guard unit "has orders" and we have not 
prioritized modeling effects against units below starting strength.

#### How can you help?

Load up your roster and take a look at the Units and Rules pages. Every rule shows how they are modelled. 
If you see something off about a rule or your detachment, hit the "Report Issue" button.  The rule gets reported
as modelled wrong and its' automatically triaged and flagged for me to review.  You are free to explain the issue 
or just leave it blank and I'll review the rule myself.  

Thanks for your help and good hunting!
