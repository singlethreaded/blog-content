A couple of weeks ago I [shared a progress report](/blog/2026-06-15-rule-coverage-progress) on how
much of 11th edition Fatecaster actually modeled: 63% of damage-affecting rules fully modeled, 93% at
least partially, and a build list of the hard cases that were left.

That build list is now cleared. **Every damage-affecting rule in 11th edition is modeled and ready to
use.** Unit abilities, army rules, detachment rules, stratagems — if a rule changes the attack math,
the engine is accounting for it. The edition is in.

<Metric
  label="Damage-affecting rules modeled"
  value="100%"
  caption="Unit abilities, army rules, detachment rules, and stratagems across all 43 factions"
/>

I want to be straight about what "modeled" means, because trust in the numbers is the whole point.
A handful of rules are still deliberately *approximated* — narrow sub-clauses I've judged either too
rare or too situational to move a real result (we still don't care whether a Guard unit "has orders",
for instance). Where that's true, it's a conscious modeling decision, not a gap. The core effect of
every rule that bends the dice is in the math.

## The proof: edition-wide rankings

Talk is cheap, so here's the receipt. I've taken the finished engine and run it across the *entire*
11th edition catalog to produce a single, edition-wide power ranking of every unit.

<Callout tone="success" title="See it live">
  The rankings are up right now at [fatecaster.io/rankings](https://fatecaster.io/rankings) — no login
  needed to look.
</Callout>

This only exists because the modeling is complete. You can't honestly rank a Custodes Caladius against
a Tyranid Exocrine if half of either faction's rules are missing from the math. Ranking the whole
edition at once is the thing that only became possible once the coverage hit 100% — which is exactly
why it's the proof I wanted to ship alongside this milestone.

## Where you come in

I'd genuinely love for the community to kick the tires on this — especially **faction experts**.

You know your faction's quirks better than any audit agent does. So my one ask: **open your faction's
ranking and give it a once-over.** Does the order feel right? Is a unit ranked way above or below where
your table experience puts it? That gut-check from someone who actually plays the faction is worth more
than another pass from me.

And if something looks off, I made it easy to tell me. Open any unit in the rankings and hit the
**Report issue** button — it flags that exact unit for my review, with the context attached, so I can
dig into whether it's a modeling bug or just a surprising-but-correct result. Both outcomes are useful;
sometimes the math is right and the surprise is the insight.

This is open beta, the rankings are free to browse, and the whole thing gets better the more eyes are
on it. Thanks for building — and now sanity-checking — your lists with me.
